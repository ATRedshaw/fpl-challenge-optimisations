How it works

The approach and reading the results

The pipeline

Three stages run in sequence to produce a predicted optimal lineup before every challenge.

Stage 1
ML Forecasting
A private model produces per-player xPts forecasts from match context and historical performance features.
Stage 2
Rule Adjustment
Raw xPts are adjusted for each challenge's scoring rules, from simple multipliers through to Poisson-modelled event probabilities and probabilistic win weights.
Stage 3
LP Optimisation
An integer linear programme selects the squad that maximises adjusted xPts within the challenge's constraints: budget, position limits and club caps.

The hindsight benchmark

Once a gameweek's scores are confirmed, the same LP solver runs again using actual points rather than forecasts. The result is the true with-hindsight optimal: the best possible lineup anyone could have picked with perfect foreknowledge. Which, to be fair, is cheating.

It is an unreachable ceiling by design. No pre-gameweek prediction can match it, but it gives an honest upper bound to measure against. Players appearing in both lineups are highlighted in green inside each challenge view.

Reading the match rate

Match rate is the share of players the predicted lineup has in common with the hindsight-optimal. The number will look low, and that is expected. Each challenge has its own squad format and size, but the eligible player pool is always around 600. The number of valid squad combinations is astronomically large regardless of how many players you are picking. Getting any meaningful fraction of the optimal selection right before a ball has been kicked is a genuinely good result.

A 50% match rate means half the predicted squad matched the best possible selection chosen from roughly 600 players. The probability of landing that by chance is effectively zero. The model is finding real signal.

The points gap matters more

Player overlap only tells part of the story. Two completely different squads can score nearly identical points when the market has priced in the same likely contributors. The more telling measure is how close the predicted lineup's actual score comes to the hindsight maximum.

Open any completed challenge to see the predicted xPts alongside the actual score for each lineup, and the points gap to the hindsight optimal in the comparison panel at the bottom.
